Tractor hire is one of the most important services in Kenyan farming — it determines whether your land is prepared on time and your planting window is met. Here's how tractor hiring works, current rates, and how to find a reliable operator.

## Tractor Hire Services Available

Most tractor hire services in Kenya offer:

- **Primary ploughing (disc plough):** Initial land opening, 20–30cm deep
- **Secondary tillage / harrowing:** Breaking clods, levelling, seedbed preparation
- **Ripping:** Busting hardpan on compacted soils (deeper, 40–50cm)
- **Subsoiling:** Breaking deep hardpan — important in areas with long-term compaction
- **Planting:** Tractor-mounted planters for maize, sunflower, wheat
- **Spray boom:** Chemical application on large fields

## Current Tractor Hire Rates (2025/2026)

Rates vary by region, fuel prices, and service type. These are indicative ranges:

| Service | Rate per Acre | Rate per Hectare |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Primary ploughing | KES 2,500–4,000 | KES 6,000–10,000 |
| Harrowing | KES 1,500–2,500 | KES 3,700–6,200 |
| Ripping | KES 3,000–5,000 | KES 7,500–12,500 |
| Planting (maize) | KES 1,500–2,500 | KES 3,700–6,200 |
| Full package (plough + harrow + plant) | KES 6,000–9,000 | KES 15,000–22,000 |

**Rates are higher for:**
- Rocky or virgin land
- Wet season when soil is heavy
- Areas far from main roads (transport cost added)
- Urgent bookings during peak season

## Booking Tractor Hire: What to Ask

Before you pay, confirm:

1. **What tractor model and size?** A 65HP tractor is suitable for 5–20 acres. For heavy soils or large areas (50+ acres), you want 85HP+.
2. **What implements are included?** Disc plough, offset disc harrow, ripper? Make sure it's what you need.
3. **What's the rate — per acre or per day?** Per acre is usually fairer for the farmer. Per day favours the operator if work is slow.
4. **Who pays for fuel?** In Kenya, most operators include fuel in their per-acre rate. Confirm this.
5. **What's the cancellation policy?** If it rains and they can't work, who bears the cost?
6. **Do they have a depth control?** Ask the operator to demonstrate that they can maintain consistent ploughing depth.

## When to Book

The biggest mistake Kenyan farmers make is booking too late.

- **Long rains season (March planting):** Book tractor in January–February. By March, every tractor is fully booked and you'll wait weeks or miss your planting window.
- **Short rains season (October planting):** Book in August–September.

Booking early also gives you negotiating power on price — operators give better rates when they're not already overbooked.

## County Government Tractors

Most Kenyan counties have subsidised tractor hire services through the county agriculture department. These are typically **30–50% cheaper** than private hire.

**Challenges:** Long waitlists, sometimes poor maintenance, fixed schedule.

**How to access:** Visit your county agriculture office and register. Confirm availability and schedule well in advance.

## Common Problems and How to Avoid Them

**Problem: Operator disappears after receiving deposit**
- Pay minimum deposit upfront (25–30%)
- Use a written agreement or at minimum a text confirmation
- Hire through recommendation from a trusted neighbour or cooperative

**Problem: Poor ploughing quality (too shallow, uneven depth)**
- Supervise the work
- Check depth periodically — insert a stick to measure
- Agree on depth (20cm for most crops) before work starts

**Problem: Operator breaks down mid-field**
- Ask if they have backup or can get repairs quickly
- Old, poorly maintained tractors break frequently during peak season

**Problem: Per-day billing inflated**
- Prefer per-acre rates
- If paying per day, agree on daily acreage target in writing

## Finding Tractor Hire in Kenya

- **Ask neighbouring farmers** — word of mouth is the most reliable
- **Your local agro-vet** — usually knows operators in the area
- **County agriculture extension officer**
- **Agrisoko services listings** — [browse equipment hire services](/browse/services) listed by operators in your county
